This is the center hill from about the middle (height-wise) of the basin. Climbing it was more or less walking until it became exceptionally steep, and then the technique was a mix of kicking into the hill with the cleats and stabbing it with hands to drag myself up or to the side. Mentally I refer to it as 'spider climbing' although I'm sure there's a real name for it.

Hole leading to the lake. For some reason this was completely uninteresting to the photographers, although I found it cool. There were lots of mini ones of these, sometimes leading to rocks or just a hole higher up. I think climbers die in crevasses sometimes. They really stood here the whole time, barely talking and with no music. I'd be so bored but they're clearly invested in the process.
